Why Your New Phone Box Feels Empty (No Charger? No Earphones? Here’s Why!)

Published: October 25, 2025

🧠 1. The “Eco-Friendly” Claim

What they say:

“We’re reducing e-waste and saving the planet 🌍.”

The facts:

  • Smaller boxes = less shipping emissions.
  • Less plastic & packaging waste.
  • But if you buy a charger separately, the benefit shrinks.

🔗 Samsung’s official statement

🔗 Apple’s explanation

💸 2. The Hidden Business Advantage

What’s really happening:

  • Cutting adapters saves companies billions in manufacturing & shipping.
  • Smaller boxes = more phones per shipment.
  • You end up buying the charger separately — boosting profits.

🔗 PhoneArena: Apple saved billions

🌐 3. Copycat Effect

Once Apple started the trend in 2020, others followed:

  • Samsung → Galaxy S21 (no charger).
  • Xiaomi, Oppo, Honor joined soon after.
  • EU’s USB-C law encourages standard ports, not included chargers.

🔗 4imag.com – Charger disappearance explained

⚡ 4. What You Lose as a Buyer

  • You may need to buy fast-charging adapters separately.
  • Not all old chargers support 45W or 65W speeds.
  • Some fast-charging systems only work with specific cables.

📢 **Tip:** Always check the box contents before purchase — even for refurbished devices.

💡 5. Smart Shopping Tips (From Deenice Finds)

  • ✅ Keep your old chargers (especially USB-C PD).
  • ✅ Buy certified fast chargers (GaN if possible).
  • ✅ Look for bundle deals that include accessories.
  • ✅ If selling/refurbishing, list what’s included clearly.

🏁 Bottom Line

Removing chargers might look “green,” but it’s also very profitable.

Smaller boxes mean big savings for brands — and extra costs for you.

Stay informed, buy smart, and reuse where you can.
